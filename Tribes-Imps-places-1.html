Tribes of Odisha

Kondh

Kondh is one of the largest tribes in Odisha. They worship hills, nature, and streams. Kondh people have deep knowledge of mountains, trees, forests, and plants.

Sauras

Sauras is an ancient tribe in India, mentioned in Hindu epics like Mahabharata and Ramayana. They have a unique shamanic culture and are skilled craftsmen, famous for Saura paintings.

Bonda

Bonda is one of the country's primitive tribes living in the hills of Malkangiri, Odisha. Their unique culture, customs, and traditional attire make them very popular.

Santhals

Santhals are the third largest tribe in India. They were great fighters during British rule and love music and dance. They are known for being fun-loving and lively.

Gonds

Gonds are a warrior tribe living in the hills of Koraput, Balangir, Sunderbans, Kalahandi, and Sambalpur. They are one of the largest tribes in South Asia.

Bhumias

Bhumia is a celebrated tribe in Odisha, rich in tradition and culture. Their unique marriage customs and rituals make them special.

Oraons

Oraons are considered one of the most progressive tribes. Many of them work in tea plantations, and many have moved to metro cities for work.

Koyas

Koyas are one of the top tribes in Odisha, known for their rich traditions, culture, and art. They mainly live in Malkangiri, Odisha, but some also live in Andhra Pradesh and Chhattisgarh.

Parajas

Parajas live in forests and hills and worship many gods and goddesses. They love music and dance and mainly work in agriculture.

Gadav

Gadav is one of the oldest tribes in Odisha, tracing history back to the Ramayana era. They are colorful and live through agriculture, hunting, and fishing.

Juanga

Juanga people speak a dialect of the Munda language. They celebrate festivals and marriages with Changu dance. Early marriages are common, which sometimes causes health issues for children born to young parents.

Important Lakes

Chilika Lake - Asia's largest brackish water lagoon

Anshupa Lake - Odisha's largest freshwater lake

Kanjia Lake - Located on the northern outskirts of Bhubaneswar


Important Power Plants

Talcher Thermal Power Station - Coal-fired, second largest power plant in India

IB Thermal Power Station - Located in Jharsuguda district

Hirakud Power Plant - Located in Sambalpur district


National Parks, Bird and Wildlife Sanctuaries

Simlipal National Park - National park and tiger reserve in Mayurbhanj district

Bhitarkanika National Park - Located in Kendrapara district

Satkosia Tiger Reserve - Tiger reserve in Angul district

Hadgarh Wildlife Sanctuary - Located in Keonjhar and Mayurbhanj districts

Nandankanan - Adjacent to Chandaka-Dampara Wildlife Sanctuary, near Kanjia Lake
