Odisha At a Glance - 2

Revenue Divisions of Odisha

Odisha has 30 districts, which are divided into three revenue divisions to ensure smooth administration.
These divisions are Central Division, Northern Division, and Southern Division.

Central Division has its headquarters at Cuttack.
Northern Division has its headquarters at Sambalpur.
Southern Division has its headquarters at Berhampur.

Each revenue division consists of 10 districts.

Governor and State Government of Odisha

Odisha has a Governor and a Chief Minister who lead the state administration.

Odisha Legislative Assembly

The Odisha Legislative Assembly is a unicameral (single-house) legislature.
It is located in Bhubaneswar, the capital of Odisha.

The Assembly has a total of 147 Members of Legislative Assembly (MLAs). Out of these:
33 seats are reserved for Scheduled Tribes (ST).
24 seats are reserved for Scheduled Castes (SC).

The Secretariat building of Odisha in Bhubaneswar is called Lok Seva Bhavan, as announced by Chief Minister Naveen Patnaik.

Governor of Odisha

The present Governor of Odisha is Kambhampati Hari Babu.
Sir John Austen Hubback was the First Governor of Odisha.

State Emblem of Odisha

On 3 August 1964, the Council of Ministers approved the Konark Horse statue as the State Emblem of Odisha.
The emblem represents discipline, strength, and progress.

It is a circular seal showing the warrior and horse statue of the Konark Sun Temple.
The Lion Capital of Ashoka appears at the top of the emblem.

High Court of Odisha

Earlier, the Bengal Presidency included Assam, Bihar, Jharkhand, Orissa, and West Bengal.

On 1 April 1936, Orissa became a separate province, but it did not have its own High Court.

Later, the Government of India decided to establish a High Court and issued the Orissa High Court Order, 1948 on 30 April 1948 under Section 229(1) of the Government of India Act, 1935.

The Orissa High Court was formally inaugurated on 26 July 1948.

Present Chief Justice of Odisha - Dr. Justice Bidyut Ranjan Sarangi.

Bira Kishore Ray (26 July 1948 – 30 October 1951) was an Indian judge who was the first Chief Justice of Orissa High Court.


Some Important Points

  • As per the 2011 Census of India, the total number of households in Odisha was 9,661,085.
  • Based on the 2011 Census data, the total population of Odisha was 41,974,218.
  • According to the 2011 Census of India, the male population of Odisha was 21,212,136.
  • As per Census 2011, the total female population of Odisha was 20,762,082.
  • Odisha’s child population in the age group of 0–6 years was 5,273,194, as recorded in the 2011 Census.
  • The population density of Odisha was 270 persons per square kilometre according to the 2011 Census.
  • As per the 2011 Census, Odisha’s sex ratio was 979 females per 1,000 males.
  • The literacy rate of Odisha as per the Census of India 2011 was 72.87%.
  • Male literacy rate in Odisha, according to Census 2011, was 81.59%.
  • Female literacy rate in Odisha as per the 2011 Census was 64.01%.
  • As per Census 2011, Scheduled Tribes (ST) constituted 22.85% of the total population of Odisha.
  • According to the 2011 Census of India, Scheduled Castes (SC) formed 17.13% of Odisha’s population.
